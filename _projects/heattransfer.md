A homework assignment from heat transfer that I found to be really valuable was the fourth problem set we received. At that point in the class, we had begun discussing fins, their use, and how to model them. Question 2 of this problem set was illuminating in how it revealed the strength of engineering models as opposed to pure mathematical ones. The question asks a student to consider the temperature distribution of a fin with partial insulation near the base. My first instinct when approaching this problem was to make a differential equation with specific heat transfer conditions (conduction only in the insulated portion, convective heat loss in the non-insulated portion), however the difficulty of this approach grew rapidly.

A much simpler approach is detailed in the homework, which models the fin as an equivalent resistor network. The insulated portion forms one resistor, and the non-insulated portion forms another resistor. Using an existing expression for fin resistance, computing things such as the temperature profile becomes incredibly simple, down to consulting tables.
